  • Designed and implemented a comprehensive benchmark to evaluate design pattern detection methods (based on automatic generation of testbeds)

    Duration: February 2017 – January 2019

    PDB_GTGT is the result of a novel research conducted at the SQLab towards benchmarking in the field of design pattern detection. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first benchmark which uses automatic generation of testbeds for the evaluation process. PDB_GTGT has a precise, and repeatable evaluation process, and can be used to compare pattern detection methods in an objective and fair fashion. In this benchmark, a detection method is evaluated based on its ability to mine different types of patterns (and their variants) from testbeds with different levels of complexity. PDB_GTGT meets the desiderata for benchmarks. It is fair, expandable, accessible, cost-effective, solvable, portable, and scalable.

  • Designed and implemented a method for the precise detection of code smells/anti-patterns from source code (based on quality metrics and software refactoring opportunities)

    Duration: December 2017 – September 2018

    Bad smells (anti-patterns) are bad practices in the design and implementation of a software system. These bad solutions significantly affect the maintainability and understandability of source code. Therefore, detection of bad smells plays an important role in the refactoring, and maintaining of large and complex software systems. However, issues such as informal specifications of bad smells and high false positive rates in the detection process complicate the detection process. In this project, we have developed and implemented a new method to detect bad smells in code by addressing the aforementioned issues.

  • Designed and implemented a method for the efficient and precise detection of software design patterns from source code (based on graph theory and method signatures)

    Duration: September 2015 – June 2016

    In this project, we have developed and implemented a new design pattern detection method based on the graph theory. The proposed detection process is subdivided into two sequential phases. In the first phase, we concern both the semantics and the syntax of the structural signature of patterns. To do so, the system under study and the patterns asked to be detected, are transformed into semantic graphs. Now, the initial problem is converted into the problem of finding matches in the system graph for the pattern graph. To reduce the exploration space, based on a predetermined set of criteria, the system graph is broken into the possible subsystem graphs. After applying a semantic matching algorithm and obtaining the candidate instances, by analyzing the behavioral signature of the patterns, in the second phase, final matches will be obtained.

    This paper describes the system.

  • Implemented a method to detect retinal micro-aneurysms using multi-scale correlation coefficients

    Duration: October 2014 – January 2015

    The diabetic retina is a serious and common complication of long-term diabetes in individuals. This complication can damage the retina and ultimately lead to blindness. Since, the emergence of red lesions (microaneurysms) on the retina is one of the first symptoms of a diabetic retina, extensive research has been done on the effective detection of these abnormalities. In this project, we have implemented a method based on the multi-scale correlation filtering and dynamic thresholding. This method involves two steps: (1) detection of candidate red lesions, and (2) precise detection of red lesions. The effectiveness of the method is evaluated on ROC dataset.

  • Implemented a method for the automatic detection of diabetic retinopathy exudates from fundus images using mathematical morphology methods

    Duration: February 2015 – July 2015

    Diabetic retinopathy, caused by changes in the blood vessels of the retina, is a common complication of diabetes. This disease results in blurred or distorted vision and is one of the main causes of blindness in the world. Exudate is one of the early symptoms of diabetic retinopathy. Ophthalmologists require dilation of pupil's eye to detect exudates. Dilation of pupil, which is performed using a chemical solution, is a painful act and consumes lot of time. In this project, based on morphological operations, we have detected exudates from fundus images in the STARE bank automatically.

  • Implemented an Optical Character Recognition method to identify Persian-typed digits

    Duration: October October 2014 – January 2015

    Optical character recognition (OCR) is the process of recognizing characters automatically in images and converting them into texts in a way that they can be searched and edited by a computer. An OCR system consists of several parts. First, the input image should be analyzed and modified if it’s text is rotated. After correcting the rotation, the position of the text, figure, and table blocks should be specified in the image. After determining the position of these kind of blocks (analyzing the document structure), the text blocks must be recognized; i.e. the text lines are found, and then the position of the words is determined, and in the next step, the position of the letters will be specified in the word. In this project, we have implemented an OCR system to identify Persian digits. For this purpose, we have used torque constants and a Fourier descriptor model for the extraction of the features.
